About Andy Warhol's Death and Disasters series

In 1962 Warhol began reproducing newspaper images in a series of paintings he called Death and Disasters. The images include car wrecks, plane crashes, or an electric chair. The series is not about violence so much as the power of the media and the way that, as Warhol said, “when you see a gruesome picture over and over again, it really doesn’t have any effect.”
(From the Boston Museum of Fine Arts website)
